Hello!

Hi, I'm Sam! I am a former educator and gardener, current bookseller and student, and future librarian. I'm pursuing a Master's of Library and Information Science at Pratt Institute, and am interested in children's librarianship, GIS/mapping, and reference. I grew up in Silver Spring, Maryland, just outside of Washington, DC. I love my home town and have lots of MD and DC pride, but have also been shaped by time spent in western Massachussetts; Street, MD (northeast of Baltimore); southern and southwestern France; and New York! At the moment my main hobbies are browsing Facebook Marketplace for tchotchkes, keeping up my DuoLingo Yiddish streak (1158 days and counting!), and searching for the best gluten-free bagel NYC has to offer. I also love spending time outside, reading, and crafting.

My Academic Interests

I hold a bachelor's degree in French and Geography, and am bringing thos experiences into my LIS studies. Below are some of my academic interests:

  1. Francophone Language, Literature, and Culture
    • translation, particularly the act of translation as a way of relating to language, place, and history more braodly
    • women's autobiographies and memoirs, specifically the work of Nobel Prize Winner Annie Ernaux
    • francophone Senegalese writing, and the way many authors' works explore the political and social relationship between France and the places it colonized
  2. Food Systems
    • smallholder agriculture as the intersection of national identity and social and environmental policy
    • supply chains and sustainability in urban farming and gardening
    • food access and education and its relationship to building youth and community agency
  3. Mapping and Cartography
    • critical cartography and its potential for shifting representations of power
    • the role of archives and mapping in community and public history projects
    • participatory GIS and its role in addressing natural disasters
